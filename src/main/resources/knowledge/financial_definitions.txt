FINANCIAL DEFINITIONS & TERMINOLOGY

PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT:
- Portfolio: A collection of investments (assets) held by a client or investor
- Asset: An individual investment instrument (stock, commodity, mutual fund, bond)
- Holding: A specific position in an asset (quantity and purchase price)
- Asset Under Management (AUM): Total market value of all assets in a portfolio
- Net Worth: Total portfolio value minus any liabilities

PERFORMANCE METRICS:
- Profit & Loss (P&L): The difference between current value and cost basis
- Unrealized P&L: Gains or losses on positions that haven't been sold yet
- Realized P&L: Gains or losses from completed trades (sold positions)
- Cost Basis: The original purchase price of an asset
- Market Value: Current market price Ã— quantity held
- Return on Investment (ROI): Percentage gain or loss relative to initial investment

RISK METRICS:
- Beta: Measure of portfolio volatility relative to market benchmark
  * Beta = 1.0: Moves with market average
  * Beta > 1.0: More volatile than market (aggressive)
  * Beta < 1.0: Less volatile than market (conservative)
- Value at Risk (VaR): Maximum potential loss at a given confidence level (e.g., 95%)
- Volatility: Measure of price fluctuations over time
- Standard Deviation: Statistical measure of price variability

ASSET CATEGORIES:
- NSE (National Stock Exchange): Equity stocks traded on Indian stock exchange
- MF (Mutual Fund): Pooled investment funds managed by professional managers
- COMMODITY: Physical goods (gold, silver, oil, agricultural products)

RISK CONCEPTS:
- Concentration Risk: Over-exposure to a single asset, sector, or category
- Diversification: Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk
- Correlation: How assets move relative to each other
- Liquidity Risk: Difficulty selling an asset without significant price impact
- Market Risk: Risk of losses due to overall market movements

PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT CONCEPTS:
- Rebalancing: Adjusting portfolio allocation to maintain target percentages
- Asset Allocation: Distribution of investments across different asset categories
- Dollar-Cost Averaging: Investing fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- Buy and Hold: Long-term investment strategy without frequent trading
